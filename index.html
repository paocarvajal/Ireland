The 'Our Living Islands' Policy: A Deep Dive

This 10-year government strategy is designed to boost life on approximately 30 offshore, non-bridged islands. Notable examples include Arranmore (Co. Donegal), Clare Island (Co. Mayo), and Inis Mór (one of the Aran Islands). The core of the plan for us is the grant system:

Vacant Property Grant: Up to €60,000

This is the base grant. To qualify, you must buy a home that was built before 2008 and has been vacant for at least two years.

Derelict Property Top-Up: Up to €24,000

If the property is confirmed to be structurally unsound and dangerous (derelict), you can get this additional amount, bringing the potential total to €84,000.

What the Grant Covers:

The money is for major works to make the house habitable, not for decoration. Think:

  • Structural repairs (roof, walls, foundations)
  • Insulation and damp-proofing
  • Plumbing, sewerage, and electrical work
  • Window and door replacement

Crucial Caveat

You must own the property before applying. The grant is paid out in stages after you've paid contractors and submitted receipts. This means you need significant capital upfront for both the purchase and the initial renovation phases.

Path to Residency: A Detailed Breakdown

For the German Citizen

As an EU citizen, your right to live and work in Ireland is protected by EU freedom of movement. Your path is straightforward.

Key Steps Post-Arrival:
  • No visa required.
  • After 90 days, you should be engaged in work or study to establish residency formally.
  • Apply for a Personal Public Service (PPS) Number, which is essential for taxes, services, and employment.
  • Open an Irish bank account.

For the Mexican Citizen

This is the primary hurdle. You must secure a long-stay visa independently. The property grant offers no support for this. An immigration lawyer is non-negotiable.

Option 1: Critical Skills Employment Permit

This is the most common route. It's for professionals in high-demand fields.

Requirements:

  • A confirmed 2-year job offer from an Irish company.
  • The role must be on the Critical Skills Occupations List (e.g., IT, Engineering, Finance, Health).
  • Minimum annual salary of €38,000 or more, depending on the role.
Option 2: Start-up Entrepreneur Programme (STEP)

For individuals with an innovative business idea.

Requirements:

  • A detailed, high-potential business plan.
  • Access to €50,000 in secured funding.
  • The business must be innovative and capable of creating 10 jobs in Ireland.

Note: The Immigrant Investor Programme (IIP) was closed in 2023 and is no longer an option.

Expanded Risk & Mitigation Plan

RISK: Immigration Denial (Mexican Citizen)

MITIGATION: This is the 'project killer'. Do not spend any money on property search trips or legal fees for property until you have a written, approved visa or a very high degree of confidence from an immigration lawyer. The entire project hinges on this.

RISK: Budget Overruns & Island Logistics

MITIGATION: Island construction is more expensive. Materials arrive by ferry, and contractor availability is limited. Get island-specific quotes. Your contingency fund should be closer to 30%. Build a strong relationship with a local project manager.

RISK: Cultural & Social Isolation

MITIGATION: Small island communities are tight-knit. Your arrival will be noticed. Visit multiple times, especially during a wet, windy winter. Talk to locals. Understand the social dynamics. Are you ready for stormy days when the ferry doesn't run?
